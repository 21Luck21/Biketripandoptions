The Cape Arkona run

~100 km · side & tailwind
Train straight to Sassnitz, then a long northbound day to the cape — Jasmund cliffs, the Schaabe sandbar, Wittow heath, and the lighthouses at Arkona. Ferry back, finish in Bergen.
  1. 07:30
    Train Berlin → Stralsund → Sassnitz (RE9 + RB), bikes on board.
  2. 11:30
    Sassnitz → Lohme → Glowe along the Jasmund cliff road · WNW wind sideways.
  3. 13:30
    Cross the Schaabe sandbar to Juliusruh · sea on both sides, easy spinning.
  4. 15:00
    Cape Arkona: twin lighthouses, Slavic Jaromarsburg earthworks, lunch at the cape.
  5. 17:00
    South via Wiek → Wittow Ferry → Trent · tailwind back across the Bodden.
  6. 19:30
    Camp near Bergen · cold beer, hot dinner.

Mönchgut & the long tailwind

~100 km · tailwind east & SE
From Bergen straight into the wind's favour: south-east through Putbus, the pier towns, Mönchgut's tip at Thiessow, then north up the Schmale Heide back to the Sassnitz train.
  1. 07:30
    Coffee, pack camp in Bergen.
  2. 08:30
    South to Putbus (the White Town) and Lauterbach's harbour.
  3. 10:30
    East along the bay to Sellin's white pier · second breakfast on the boardwalk.
  4. 12:30
    Baabe → Göhren → Thiessow: loop the Mönchgut tip · fishing villages, dunes, swim.
  5. 15:30
    North along the Schmale Heide sandbar through Binz → Prora → Mukran.
  6. 17:30
    Roll into Sassnitz · train home via Stralsund · Rotspon at Berlin Hbf.

Wind & weather check

Let the wind decide.

On Rügen, check wind direction the night before — start north (Sassnitz/Lohme) on a southerly, south (Sellin/Baabe) on a northerly. The whole island works in either direction.

In Brandenburg, the dark-sky run wants a clear, dry forecast — check wetter.de.

Wildcamping rules

Sleep light, leave nothing.

  • ·Rügen:
    only at official campsites — many along the coast.
